ingredients:
  - 1 lb lean ground beef (90% lean or higher)
  - 1 cup long-grain brown rice (uncooked)
  - 1 1/2 cups low-sodium beef broth or water
  - 1 small onion, diced
  - 1 red bell pepper, diced
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  - 1 tsp chili powder
  - 1 tsp cumin
  - 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  - 1/4 tsp black pepper
  - 1/2 tsp sea salt (adjust to taste)
  - 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (optional)
  - Lime wedges for serving
directions:
  - In a large skillet or sauté pan over medium heat, cook the ground beef until browned, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  - Add onion, bell pepper, and garlic to the skillet and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  - Stir in uncooked rice, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  - Pour in beef broth or water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  - Remove from heat and let sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff rice with a fork.
  - Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges.
---

A **quick, one-pot, protein-packed meal** that’s low in calories but high in flavor. Lean ground beef provides protein, while brown rice and vegetables add fiber and nutrients — perfect for an easy weeknight dinner or meal prep.
